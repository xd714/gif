Welcome

I am a PhD researcher specializing in computational animal breeding and genetics, with a particular focus on genetic parameter identifiability and body weight prediction models. My work combines advanced statistical modeling, machine learning techniques, and genomic analysis to improve livestock breeding programs and address complex challenges in quantitative genetics.

Currently based at the Leibniz Institute for Farm Animal Biology (FBN) in Germany, I am passionate about applying computational methods to solve real-world challenges in animal agriculture and sustainable food production. My recent research on genetic parameter identifiability in massively deep pedigrees contributes to more accurate breeding value estimation and improved breeding program efficiency.
