Welcome

I am a PhD candidate specializing in computational animal breeding and genetics at the Leibniz Institute for Farm Animal Biology (FBN) in Germany. My research focuses on quantitative genetics, genetic parameter identifiability, and the development of computational tools for genomic analysis and breeding value estimation.

With a strong background in both theoretical and applied aspects of animal genetics, I combine advanced statistical modeling, machine learning techniques, and genomic analysis to address complex challenges in quantitative genetics. My work contributes to improving livestock breeding programs through better understanding of genetic parameters and the development of innovative computational approaches for genetic analysis.

Before my PhD, I gained practical experience in industrial plant breeding at KWS LOCHOW GmbH, where I worked on rye breeding, data management, and yield trial analysis. This diverse background in both animal and plant genetics provides me with a comprehensive perspective on agricultural breeding systems.
