About My Research

I am a PhD candidate specializing in computational animal breeding and genetics at the Leibniz Institute for Farm Animal Biology (FBN) in Germany. My research focuses on quantitative genetics, genetic parameter identifiability, and the development of computational tools for genomic analysis and breeding value estimation.

With expertise spanning statistical modeling, machine learning, and genomic analysis, I address complex challenges in quantitative genetics to improve livestock breeding programs. My work contributes to sustainable food production through better understanding of genetic parameters and innovative computational approaches.

Before my PhD, I gained practical experience in industrial plant breeding at KWS LOCHOW GmbH, working on rye breeding and data analysis. This diverse background provides me with a comprehensive perspective on agricultural breeding systems.
