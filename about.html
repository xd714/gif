About Me

I am a computational researcher with a strong background in animal genetics and breeding. My interdisciplinary approach combines expertise in statistics, bioinformatics, and animal science to address complex challenges in modern livestock production.

My research focuses on developing innovative computational methods for animal breeding programs, with particular emphasis on body weight prediction and genomic selection. I am passionate about applying cutting-edge technologies to improve the efficiency and sustainability of animal agriculture.

Research Interests

  • Computational animal breeding and genetics
  • Body weight prediction models
  • Genomic selection and breeding value estimation
  • Machine learning applications in animal science
  • Statistical genetics and quantitative trait analysis

Education & Background

Currently pursuing PhD in Computational Animal Breeding and Genetics at the Leibniz Institute for Farm Animal Biology (FBN) in Germany. My doctoral research focuses on developing advanced computational methods for improving livestock breeding efficiency through data-driven approaches.

Based in Germany, I collaborate with international research teams to advance the field of computational animal science and contribute to sustainable food production systems.
