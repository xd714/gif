My Journey

My path in computational genetics began at China Agricultural University, where I studied farm animal science. An exchange program at University of Wisconsin opened my eyes to international agricultural practices.

Moving to Germany for my Master's at University of Rostock was transformative. Working with R and ASReml for statistical genetics sparked my passion for computational analysis. My thesis on mouse genetics using mixed models was a turning point.

After gaining industry experience at KWS LOCHOW in plant breeding, I returned to research for my PhD at FBN. Here I combine statistical modeling, machine learning, and genomic analysis to solve real problems in animal breeding.
