About Me

I am a computational researcher with a strong background in animal genetics and breeding, currently pursuing my PhD at the Leibniz Institute for Farm Animal Biology (FBN) in Germany. My interdisciplinary approach combines expertise in statistics, bioinformatics, and animal science to address complex challenges in modern livestock production.

My research focuses on developing innovative computational methods for animal breeding programs, with particular emphasis on genetic parameter identifiability and body weight prediction models. I am passionate about applying cutting-edge statistical and machine learning technologies to improve the efficiency and sustainability of animal agriculture.

My recent work on genetic parameter identifiability in massively deep pedigrees represents a significant contribution to the field of quantitative genetics, providing insights that enhance the accuracy of breeding value estimation and optimization of breeding programs.

Education & Background

Currently pursuing PhD in Computational Animal Breeding and Genetics at the Leibniz Institute for Farm Animal Biology (FBN) in Germany since March 2022. My doctoral research focuses on developing advanced computational methods for improving livestock breeding efficiency through data-driven approaches, with specific emphasis on genetic parameter identifiability and growth modeling.

I completed my Master's degree in Farm Animal Science at University of Rostock in 2020, where I developed a strong foundation in statistical genetics and breeding methodologies. My master's thesis focused on genetic parameter estimation in mouse populations using R Studio and ASReml for univariate and multivariate models.

My Bachelor's degree in Farm Animal Science was obtained from China Agricultural University in Beijing (2016), where I also participated in an exchange program at the University of Wisconsin River Falls, USA. This international experience broadened my perspective on global agricultural practices and research methodologies.

Based in Germany, I collaborate with international research teams to advance the field of computational animal science and contribute to sustainable food production systems. My work bridges the gap between theoretical statistical methods and practical applications in animal breeding programs.

Professional Experience

Before beginning my PhD, I gained valuable industry experience as a Breeding Technician at KWS LOCHOW GmbH, where I worked on industrial plant breeding specializing in rye. This role involved data management and analysis, yield trial coordination, field planning, and scientific reporting - experiences that complement my current research in animal genetics.

I also worked as a student researcher at FBN, focusing on piglet birth observation, sow farrowing behavior analysis, and laboratory work. This hands-on experience with animal research provided me with practical insights that inform my current computational work.

My diverse background in both plant and animal breeding, combined with computational expertise, gives me a unique perspective on agricultural genetics and breeding optimization.

Recent Achievement

Published Research

I recently published my research on genetic parameter identifiability in Animal Breeding and Genetics (2025), which investigates critical challenges in quantitative genetics using massively deep pedigree structures in mice populations. This work provides essential insights for improving breeding program accuracy and efficiency.

I also contributed to the development of CLARITY, an interactive R Shiny application for visualizing bovine genetic and physical maps, published in Frontiers in Genetics (2023).
