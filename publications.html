On the Identifiability of Genetic Parameters for Growth in Mice With a Massively Deep Pedigree

Xi Ding, A. A. Musa, Norbert Reinsch

Animal Breeding and Genetics, 2025

This study investigates the identifiability of genetic parameters in growth models using a massively deep pedigree structure in mice populations. The research addresses critical challenges in quantitative genetics regarding the reliable estimation of variance components in complex breeding populations, providing insights into parameter estimation accuracy and breeding program optimization. Our findings contribute to improved understanding of genetic parameter identifiability and its implications for breeding value estimation.

CLARITY: a Shiny app for interactive visualisation of the bovine physical-genetic map

Nina Melzer, Saber Qanbari, Xi Ding, D√∂rte Wittenburg

Frontiers in Genetics, 2023

We developed CLARITY, an interactive R Shiny application that provides a platform for users to explore bovine genetic and physical maps. The tool allows researchers to visualize the relationship between genetic and physical distances across the bovine genome, facilitating genomic research and fine-mapping studies. CLARITY is freely available online and as an R package, making genomic map visualization accessible to the broader research community.

Research Impact

My research contributes to the advancement of computational animal breeding through the development of novel statistical methods and user-friendly tools. The work on genetic parameter identifiability provides crucial insights for breeding program optimization, while the CLARITY application democratizes access to genomic visualization tools for the research community.


Future research directions focus on integrating machine learning approaches with traditional quantitative genetics methods to enhance prediction accuracy and breeding efficiency in livestock populations.
